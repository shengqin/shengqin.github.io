The through-line

I engineer the pipeline and the model — turning raw single-cell, spatial, and cell-free sequence into biological signal that biologists and clinicians actually use.

Over six-plus years across academia and biotech, I've built and shipped bioinformatics pipelines and predictive models: single-cell RNA-seq deconvolution, GATK-based variant calling, ctDNA mutation tracking, and polygenic risk scoring — in Python and R, containerized and reproducible. My work spans molecular subtyping of Hodgkin Lymphoma, liquid-biopsy assays for cardiovascular risk, and preclinical target discovery in melanoma.

Industry drug-discovery experience rounds it out: preclinical modeling, clinical PK/PD assays, and companion diagnostics across ten-plus programs. I'm now focused on building agentic AI tools that accelerate scientific discovery — combining rigorous ML-for-biology with production-quality software engineering.

Research Scientist

Stanford University Palo Alto, CA
  • Built a Hodgkin lymphoma-specific gene-expression signature matrix from single-cell RNA-seq and engineered an SVM-based deconvolution pipeline to classify 187 bulk-RNA-seq tumors; used NMF to define molecular subtypes and identified a novel subtype with poor progression-free survival, validated across PhenoCycler, Visium, and RNA-microarray data.

  • Built a circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) mutation-calling pipeline to detect and track somatic variants from plasma cfDNA, enabling noninvasive tumor genotyping and disease monitoring in Hodgkin lymphoma.
  • Developed a pipeline to quantify microbial signal from bulk RNA-seq — filtering host reads and mapping non-human reads to microbial references — to profile tumor-associated microbes and viral transcripts.
  • First-author paper in Nature Communications (2025), a first-author manuscript under review at Nature Genetics, and co-author on Nature.
